Combat Reference

Standard engagement rules for all field operatives. Covers initiative, action economy, map rules, death protocol, and resolution mechanics.

Initiative & Turn Order
Section 01 - Initiative

Determining Turn Order

Roll d20 + Finesse Highest result acts first. Ties broken by higher Finesse stat. If still tied roll d2.
  • All participants roll simultaneously. Order is set highest to lowest.
  • Players may swap their position in the turn order with another player, provided the swap does not bypass an enemy's turn.

Action Economy
Section 02 - Actions

What You Can Do On Your Turn

Each turn every agent has three resources. Actions may be taken in any order. Extra actions granted by effects or abilities are always last.

1 × Movement 1 × Main Action 1 × Minor Action
  • Dash (Main Action): Move an additional distance equal to your full movement speed.
  • Dash (Minor Action): Move an additional distance equal to half your movement speed only.
  • Environment & Player Interactions: Do not consume actions unless explicitly stated. If they do, they consume a minor action unless otherwise specified.
  • Equipping / Drawing Items: Pulling out any item with a mechanical purpose costs a minor action.
Map & Movement
Section 03 - Map Rules

Grid & Occupancy

  • All maps use a square grid. Each tile is 5 ft × 5 ft.
  • Only one entity may occupy a tile at a time.
  • An entity may attempt to pass through an occupied tile. The occupier chooses which stat to contest: Might or Finesse. Both roll d20 + that stat; higher result wins.
Incapacitation & Death
Section 04 - Death Mechanics

When HP Reaches Zero

Upon reaching 0 HP or below, the agent must immediately pass a saving throw.

Check DC 17 - Clarity or Vitality
✓ Success - Last Stand
  • Remain conscious and active.
  • Turn reduced to halved movement.
  • At the start of each subsequent turn, pass DC 14 Clarity/Vitality or fall unconscious.
  • Negative HP rule still applies (see below).
  • Agents with Corruption may tap into it to fuel their last stand if they fail a check, incuring Corruption levels equal to the points needed to succeed the save.
✗ Failure - Unconscious
  • Fall unconscious immediately.
  • Can still be targeted and attacked while down.
  • Negative HP rule applies.
NEGATIVE HP - DEATH THRESHOLD
When a downed agent's HP reaches negative half of their maximum HP, they die. (e.g. an agent with 20 max HP dies at −10.)
Unarmed Attacks & Skill Checks
Section 05 - Resolution

Dice Resolution Rules

All checks are resolved as d20 + relevant stat. All results are rounded down.

Skill Check d20 + Stat    Nat 1 = Critical Failure  |  Nat 20 = Critical Success
Unarmed Hit d20 + Might / Finesse / Skill
Unarmed Dmg 1d6 + 1/2 × Might / Finesse / Skill

When being attacked, you have a choice of 4 standard reactionary moves: Block/Dodge, Evade, Endure, and Parry.

Block/Dodge HIT d20 + Finesse / Might    On success, take no damage. 
On fail, reduce damage by 1d4 + 1/2 × Finesse / Might
EVADE HIT 5d5 + Finesse × 2    Roll against attacking HIT.
On success, take no damage. 
On fail, take full damage.
ENDURE DR 4d2 + Might × 2    Roll against attacking DMG.
Reduce damage by rolled amount.
PARRY HIT d10 + Skill × 2    Roll against attacking HIT.
On success, take no damage and perform a basic attack on the enemy.
