Missions & Terminology

How missions are structured, how mission branches work, the flow of a campaign, and the official Wishmakers Corporation glossary of anomalous phenomena terminology.

Mission Structure
Section 01 - How Missions Work

Operational Overview

  • There are 13 Mission Branches; thematic families of missions with common tones, settings, or types of anomalous activity.
  • After each successful mission, players are offered a choice of three missions to take on next.
  • Concurrent missions are permitted. Multiple teams may run separate missions simultaneously. A player may not join a second mission until their current one is complete.
  • The intended gameplay ratio is approximately 60% combat, 40% exploration/RP. In practice this may be closer to 70/30 time-wise. Individual missions may vary. Some are primarily exploration or roleplay, others primarily combat.

Official Terminology - WMC Glossary
Section 03 - Anomalous Phenomena Glossary

Review of Standard WMC Terminology

The following terms are used across all Wishmakers Corporation operational documentation. All field agents are expected to be familiar with this vocabulary prior to deployment.

Daydreamnoun - General Classification
The general term for anything anomalous. Any entity, phenomenon, object, or location that exhibits properties inconsistent with known natural law is classified as a Daydream. Not inherently hostile. Classification carries no threat implication.
Nightmarenoun - Threat Classification
A hostile or dangerous anomaly. A Nightmare is a Daydream that presents an active threat to personnel, civilians, or the stability of a containment situation. The classification is behavioural. A Daydream may become a Nightmare under specific conditions.
Wonderlandnoun - Spatial / Domain Classification
A specific category of Daydream that manifests as a location rather than an entity. This includes domains, extradimensional spaces, unusual architectural anomalies, and any anomaly better described as a place than a thing. Wonderlands may contain multiple Daydreams within them, and have their own containment procedures distinct from entity-type anomalies.
Reverienoun - Item Classification
An anomalous item produced through artificial means. Reveries are primarily manufactured by the Wishmakers Corporation through the Works process applied to contained anomalies. They may also originate from rival corporations or entities, and occasionally from humans who have experienced intense emotional events. A Reverie functions as a normal item in most respects, but carries anomalous properties and an Affinity counter that grows with use.
Corruptionnoun - Mechanical / Medical
The accumulated residue of anomalous exposure on a character. Measured on a numerical track capped by Clarity and Intellect. Corruption can be partially healed, but source totals are tracked permanently across a character's lifetime. Sufficient exposure to a single source may trigger a Unique Corruption Effect specific to that anomaly. See: Core Stats/Corruption.
Worksnoun - Operational Process
The structured process of studying, interacting with, or otherwise engaging a contained anomaly with the aim of producing a Reverie. Works are categorised as Instinct, Insight, or Technique, and success is determined by the average of the relevant paired stats measured against a d100 roll. See: Kits & Reveries/Works.
Affinitynoun - Character Progression
The measure of a character's bond with a specific Reverie, accumulated through regular use. When Affinity meets the threshold for a given Reverie, the character's base kit is modified to incorporate elements, including downsides, of the Reverie. Each Reverie tracks its own Affinity counter independently.
Breachnoun - Containment Failure Event
The event that occurs when an anomaly's breach conditions are fulfilled during Works, causing it to escape containment. A breached anomaly may impose special conditions or persistent events across future missions until it is re-contained. Not all anomalies have breach conditions. Check the individual entries.
Last Standnoun - Combat State
The reduced combat state entered when an agent reaches 0 HP and passes the DC 17 death saving throw. A Last Stand agent has halved movement and must pass a DC 14 Clarity/Vitality check at the start of each subsequent turn or fall unconscious. Negative HP still applies. See: Combat/Death.
