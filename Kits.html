Kits & Reveries

How agents are equipped, how anomalies are contained and studied, and how Reveries are produced and integrated into a character's kit. Covers Works, Daydream Mood, and Affinity progression.

Agent Kits - Base Equipment
Section 01 - Kit Structure

Building Your Kit

Every agent's capabilities are defined by their Kit. A Kit is a set of abilities derived from their Reverie or, in the case of Daydreams, their inherent anomalous nature. Kits are the primary structure through which players engage in combat.

  • Each player begins with 4 slots for kit construction.
  • Slots may be used for active skills or passives. No Ultimates at kit creation.
  • Players are encouraged to create a unique basic attack. This does not consume a kit slot.
  • Players kits may interact with any of the system's functions in any way.

Section 02 - Daydreams & Reverie Origins

Kit Source: Reverie vs. Daydream

  • Most agents are equipped with either a personal Reverie or a company-issued one. The kit is built around this item.
  • Players may instead choose to play a Daydream; a character who is themselves anomalous. In this case, the kit reflects their inherent abilities rather than a Reverie.
  • Daydream characters, depending on how unusual they are, may gain additional passives and inherent drawbacks tied to their anomalous nature.
  • Lore note: Reveries used for base kits and Reveries obtained via Works (see below) are mechanically distinct categories, though they share the same in-world origin.
Containment & Works
Section 03 - Daydream Containment

Containing an Daydream

Most missions feature a Daydream. Rather than destroying it, players may attempt to contain it. A successfully contained Daydream can be studied to potentially yield a Reverie.

  • Containment requires either clearing the Daydream (in the case of Wonderlands / domain-type anomalies) or subduing the entity directly.
  • Once contained, players may perform Works on the Daydream to attempt to obtain a Reverie.
  • Works usually require a set number of successes. The required amount varies per Daydream.
Section 04 - Works

Performing Works

Works are the process of studying or interacting with a contained Daydream. Each Daydream has a preferred Work type; using it improves success odds.

WORK TYPE / INSTINCT
Instinct
Strength (MIG) + Vitality (VIT)
Physical engagement, endurance-based study. Approaching the Daydream directly. Favoured by Daydreams that respond to presence and force.
WORK TYPE / INSIGHT
Insight
Intellect (INT) + Clarity (CLA)
Intellectual and perceptive study. Understanding Daydream patterns and behaviour. Favoured by sapient or cognitively complex Daydreams.
WORK TYPE / TECHNIQUE
Technique
Finesse (FIN) + Skill (SKL)
Precise, dexterous interaction. Technical manipulation and careful procedure. Favoured by Daydreams sensitive to approach and method.
Work Roll (AVG of appropriate stats × 2) + preference bonus   vs.   d100 or 5d20 Roll below your calculated number = Success. Roll above = Failure.
  • Using the Daydream's preferred Work type adds a bonus to your calculated number.
  • The total number of successes and failures required depends on the Daydream.
  • Some Daydreams have breach conditions. Usually a maximum number of failures allowed before the Daydream escapes containment. (e.g. 5 works required, 1 fail = breach, means all 5 must be successful.)
  • A breach causes the daydream to escape containment or otherwise unworkable. This may trigger special conditions or events on future missions, which must be completed to re-contain it.
Section 05 - Daydream Mood

Mood & What It Grants

The number of successes and failures during Works affects the Daydream's mood. Daydreams may grant different items depending on their mood at the point of successful completion.

◆Sad
More failures than successes. May still yield items.
◈Neutral
Balanced or close to baseline.
◇Happy
More successes than failures, preferred Work used.

Items may be granted under any mood state. Some Daydreams may require different moods to grant rewards.

Reveries & Affinity
Section 06 - Reveries

What Is a Reverie

A Reverie is the item obtained via Works. It is an anomalous object produced by the Wishmakers Corporation, or sometimes by rival entities, or by humans who have experienced intense emotional events.

  • A Reverie holds importance roughly comparable to a player's base kit. Power scales with the rank of the Daydream it was derived from.
  • A Reverie functions as a normal item. It can be used, carried, and equipped like any other piece of gear most of the time.
  • Each Reverie has its own Affinity Counter and requirement. Requirement difficulty typically correlates with the difficulty of the Daydream it came from.
Section 07 - Affinity

Affinity & Kit Modification

  • Affinity is built by using the Reverie continuously. Each Reverie tracks its own counter independently.
  • When a player reaches the Affinity threshold for a Reverie, their base kit is modified to incorporate elements of the Reverie, including its downsides.
  • This modification happens automatically and is retained for as long as the player has it equipped.
  • Reveries obtained from higher-ranked Daydreams have higher Affinity thresholds. They take longer to bond with, and their effects on the base kit are more significant.
Inventory Rules
Section 08 - Inventory

Carrying & Equipping Items

  • Players have infinite inventory for general items.
  • Players can only equip one Reverie at a time, and can carry 2 additional ones into missions.
  • A player can have 2 tools/consumables/items equipped at once, and can bring 3 additional ones into missions.
  • Pulling out any item with a mechanical purpose (consumables, weapons, tools) costs a Minor Action, unless the item is already equipped.
  • Items that are already "equipped" (actively held or worn) can be used without spending an action to draw them.
