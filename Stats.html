Core Stats

Character attributes, derived values, point-buy rules, and the Corruption system.

The Six Attributes
MIG - Might
Might
Raw physical strength. Governs unarmed damage, contests requiring brute force, and contributes directly to maximum HP.
▸ HP formula  ▸ Unarmed hit/dmg  ▸ Force contests
FIN - Finesse
Finesse
Speed, agility, and reflexes. Determines initiative order and base movement speed.
▸ Initiative  ▸ Movement speed  ▸ Agility checks
VIT - Vitality
Vitality
Endurance and constitution. Feeds into maximum HP and is one of the two options for the death-saving throw.
▸ HP formula  ▸ Death save (DC 18)  ▸ Stamina checks
CLA - Clarity
Clarity
Willpower, charisma, and resolve. Used for death saves and caps the Corruption bar. High Clarity means more resistance to anomalous influence.
▸ Max Corruption  ▸ Death save (DC 18)  ▸ Social checks
INT - Intellect
Intellect
Knowledge and perception. Used for identifying anomalies, recalling information, and noticing things others miss.
▸ Perception  ▸ Anomaly knowledge  ▸ Investigation
SKL - Skill
Skill
Precision, dexterity, and accumulated experience. Applies to ranged attacks, technical tasks, and fine motor work.
▸ Unarmed hit/dmg  ▸ Precision tasks  ▸ Technical checks
Point-Buy & Limits
Section 01 - Stat Distribution

Building Your Agent

  • Begin with 6 points to distribute freely across all six stats.
  • Stats may be pushed into negative values to free up additional points. Maximum total borrowed from negatives: 4 points.
  • Stat ceiling: +5. Stat floor: −4.
  • All stats begin at 0 before distribution.

Derived Values
Section 02 - Calculated Values

HP & Movement Speed

Max HP 20 + (VIT × 2) + MIG    Minimum 16 regardless of negative stats
Movement 20 + (FIN × 5) ft    Minimum 5 ft regardless of negative Finesse
  • Example HP: Agent with VIT 3, MIG 2 > 20 + (3×2) + 2 = 28 HP
  • Example Movement: Agent with FIN 2 > 20 + (2×5) = 30 ft
  • Negative stats reduce these values but the listed minimums always apply.
The Corruption System
Section 03 - Corruption

Anomalous Exposure & Corruption

Corruption represents the lasting residue of anomalous exposure. It accumulates across a character's lifetime and cannot be fully undone.

Max Corruption 100 + (CLA × 5) + (INT × 2)    Minimum 80 regardless of negative stats Thresholds at 10% / 30% / 60% / 90% of max
Example - Agent with CLA 4 and INT 5 (Max: 130)
0Clean
13Threshold 1
39Threshold 2
78Threshold 3
117Threshold 4 / Comatose at 118
  • Corruption begins at 0 and only increases through play.
  • Each threshold crossed triggers narrative and mechanical effects. Loss of sanity, altered perception, physical deterioration. Etc.
  • Every threshold passed also incurs a stacking -1 penalty to the current highest stat.
  • At over 90% of max Corruption, the character enters a comatose or worse state.
Unique Corruption Effects
  • General corruption causes psychosis, instability, weakened health, and fragile constitution. These are the baseline effects of any accumulation.
  • When a character suffers 20 or more Corruption points from the same source, they develop a Unique Corruption Effect tied to that specific Daydream.
  • Unique effects are semi-permanent. Lowering your Corruption total does not remove them. Although their effects are lessened at lower corruption levels / thresholds.
  • Corruption sources are tracked and totalled across a character's lifetime. Points healed away still count toward the cumulative lifetime total per source. (e.g. 10 points suffered, healed, then 10 more from the same anomaly = 20 total, unique effect triggers.)
